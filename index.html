Physical Activities

Outdoor activities can be fun for all ages. Going to the playground at a local park is a great way to help young children develop physically by climbing, running, and jumping. While pushing a child on the swing talk about how the force of momentum work. Talk about gravity when playing with ball.

Reading Activities

Reading to a young child can be a wonderful adventure. Picking an age appropriate book is key. Books with lots of pictures will help spark the child's imagination. When reading picture books to toddlers discuss each picture pointing out the different colors, animals, and objects on each page. Story books tell a story. For instance, After reading the story 'Jack and the Beanstalk" ask the child questions about story like What happened to the beans? or What did jack get after he claimed the beanstalk? You can also reenact scenes from the story like climbing the beanstalk, going to the market, or chopping down the beanstalk.

Activities with Food

Talk about the color and shape of food packages, is it round, square or rectangle in shape. Have the child count a number of grapes and put them into a bag for a snack later. Use the scales in the produce section of the store to weigh items and compare the amount of items vs the weight like 3 apples vs 3 lemons. Talk about which heavier or lighter.
